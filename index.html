Standard operating procedures

Professionally written SOPs I create and maintain for clients — demonstrating systems thinking and documentation expertise.

SOP-001: Email Triage & Inbox Management
Version 1.0 · Prepared by John Rod Morgado · VA / Administrative Support
Purpose:To ensure all incoming emails are managed efficiently, professionally, and within required response times while maintaining confidentiality and accuracy.
Scope:Applies to all business email accounts managed by the Virtual Assistant.
Tools:Gmail / Outlook · Google Workspace / Microsoft Office · Slack / Teams · CRM (if applicable)

Procedure

  1. Log in and check for new emails at start of shift.
  2. Categorize all emails: Urgent / Client / Customer Support / Internal / Marketing / Spam.
  3. Prioritize Urgent and Client requests above all others.
  4. Draft responses — verify grammar, tone, attachments, and recipient before sending.
  5. Archive completed email threads; flag pending items with a follow-up date.
  6. Submit end-of-day inbox summary report to executive.

Response time standards

🔴 Urgent — within 30 min 🟠 Client — within 1 hr 🟡 Customer Support — within 4 hr 🟢 General — within 24 hr

Quality checklist (before every send)

  • Correct recipient confirmed
  • Subject line is clear and professional
  • Grammar and spelling verified
  • All attachments included
  • Professional closing and signature included

Key performance indicators

95% on-time response rate Inbox Zero by EOD Zero missed urgent emails High client satisfaction
Purpose:To maintain the executive's calendar with zero conflicts, complete preparation, and proactive communication.
Scope:All meetings, appointments, and events for the executive's primary and secondary calendars.
Tools:Google Calendar / Outlook · Calendly · Zoom / Google Meet · Slack

Daily procedure

  1. Review the next 48 hours each morning for conflicts or missing agendas.
  2. Block focus time before scheduling any new meetings.
  3. Add 15-minute buffers between all external calls and meetings.
  4. Confirm attendance of all external attendees 24 hours before the meeting.
  5. Attach agenda, background documents, or briefing notes to each event.
  6. Send the executive a 1-hour reminder for every meeting.
  7. Handle reschedule requests within 2 hours of notification.

Meeting preparation checklist

  • Agenda prepared and distributed to all attendees
  • Video call link tested and included
  • Briefing document or context note attached
  • Executive notified of attendees and purpose
  • Post-meeting: notes distributed within 2 hours

Key performance indicators

Zero double-bookings 100% meetings have agenda All reschedules handled same day
Purpose:To ensure every shift begins in an organized, prepared state with all priorities identified and communicated.
Frequency:Daily — start of every shift (15 minutes)

Opening routine (first 15 minutes)

  1. Check Slack / Teams for overnight messages and urgent requests.
  2. Open email — triage and apply priority labels.
  3. Review today's full calendar and note all key meetings.
  4. Open task tracker (ClickUp / Asana / Trello) — identify today's top 3 priorities.
  5. Check any pending client deadlines due today or tomorrow.
  6. Send executive a brief "Good morning" status update (if requested by client).
  7. Begin the day's work starting with the highest-priority task.

End of shift routine

  1. Complete or escalate all urgent tasks.
  2. Bring inbox to zero or flag with action dates.
  3. Update task tracker with today's progress.
  4. Send EOD report to executive.
  5. Set tomorrow's priority list.
  6. Log out of all client systems securely.

Key performance indicators

EOD report sent daily All tasks tracked Zero missed priorities
